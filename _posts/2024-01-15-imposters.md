There is no one way to do mathematics. There is no one way to think about mathematics. There is no specific rate at which to find a solution. There is no standard way of doing mathematics. There is no standard way of learning mathematics. There is no standard way of thinking about mathematics. You want a counterexample? 

If I say the word "normal" to you, what mathematical object comes to mind? What makes this object "normal?"

If I say the word "simple", "irreducible", "constructible" and now even the word "heart"... what comes to mind? 

The point I'm trying to make is this: there is no point. There is no center. There is nothing we measure ourselves against and then measure ourselves away from. So when you fail a standardized test, and you're told that you fall two standard deviations below the mean I want you to take a deep breath, you're quite alright. 

Let's say you fail every single one of your classes from this point on. Let's say you never get an A in another math class again. 

Congratulations, you're no longer a standard.
