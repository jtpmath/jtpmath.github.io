My mission at JTPmath is to solve open problems in mathematics and display the solutions to youtube. To do this, requires work on my part, since there are many subjects I must parse and re-learn to come to more informed conlusions. 

My interest in mathematics started when I failed linear algebra the first time. I was stuck on the word "kernel" for about two years after that. I just couldn't wrap my head around why they called it a kernel, so I knew I had to keep investigating. 

Eventually I got to ring theory, where kernels began to make sense. It probably would have made even more sense if I would have seen group theory before linear algebra - or even at the same time - and then went on to rings, and then modules, and then vector spaces... actually, I should have just started with <a href="https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLyDvtSibCRwzW1gu5ADtc-8K0h29_iYac">abelian categories</a>. 

Eventually I went on to take multivariable calculus. My school called it advanced calculus. I didn't make the mistake of letting myself fail this time, but I was stuck on the word "manifold." I didn't see the word manifold until a couple of years later when I finally got to graduate school. I should have just started with differential topology. 

Now I have this website, where I can start with whatever subject I want, and translate between whatever field I want, without someone telling me "I'm an algebra person" or "all of the analysis people think like this." Now there is no box to constrain my thought process and I hope that my solutions open your mind too. 


But if you *had* to assign a label: my research interests are in analytic geometry, algebraic topology and complex analysis. 

If you're curious, you can read us on [reddit](http://reddit.com/r/jtpmath).
